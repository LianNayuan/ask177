# Heavy Splatling

It is capable of firing bursts of ink with long range, low damage, and a fast fire rate. Although relatively slow to charge, each ink bullet does enough damage to splat opponents in four hits.

## Appearance

The Heavy Splatling varies in appearance between games. In Splatoon , the Heavy Splatling consists of multiple black barrels with orange supports connected to a large water jug that changes color to match the user's ink. In Splatoon 2 , the barrels are less blocky, the supports are yellow, and the water jug is at a diagonal angle instead of being completely horizontal. In Splatoon 3 , the supports are pink and gray, and a support was added to the water jug.

## Trivia

In Splatoon The Heavy Splatling was first revealed when Splatoon was datamined on 21 June 2015 .

The Slosher and Heavy Splatling were the first entirely new weapons added into the Splatoon franchise. They made their debut on 6 August 2015 at 02:00 UTC , with the release of version 2.0.0 .

In Splatoon 3 The Heavy Splatling is one of the weapons that was available to use in the Splatoon 3 Splatfest World Premiere .

The Heavy Splatling's Tableturf card depicts the victory dance performed by a player using the weapon in the original Splatoon . This animation would later return as part of the "Reppin' Inkopolis" emote .

The Heavy Splatling and the Slosher are the only weapons not made by the Splat brand to have their design changed every Splatoon game.

## Etymology

Heavy Splatling is derived from the adjective Heavy and a portmanteau of Splat and Gatling .

バレルスピナー bareru supinā is derived from Barrel and Spinner . The name Barrel Spinner is because the barrels of the Heavy Splatling rotate while charging and firing.
