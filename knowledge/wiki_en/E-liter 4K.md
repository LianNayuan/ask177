Like most chargers, this weapon can store a fully charged shot in swim form for a short amount of time, and a fully charged shot will pierce through opponents.

## Appearance

In general, the E-liter 4K is based on a plastic gas pump handle with a metallic ink tank on the back and an elongated metallic spout with a plastic attachment and a black sight which acts as the barrel.

In Splatoon 2 , the plastic parts are yellow and the ink tank changes color to match the user's ink.

In Splatoon 3 , the plastic parts are blue, the handle also has a seven-segment LCD display, and the tube connecting the handle to the ink tank and a spring on the barrel change color to match the user's ink.

## Trivia

The E-liter 4K is designed to look like a gas pump.

The E-liter 4K and its variants are created by Custom .

The E-liter 4K was one of the weapons that was available to use in the Splatoon 3 Splatfest World Premiere .

For Judd's trial on the Ink Mine , the player is given the E-Liter 4K.

Across all three games, the E-Liter 4K ( 3K in Splatoon ) has the same special as the Custom Splattershot Jr.

## Etymology

E-liter is a pun on elite and liter (the metric unit of volume by which gasoline is sold in many countries). The 4K in the name refers to the number of fully charged shots the weapon can fire before running out of ink, which is four.
