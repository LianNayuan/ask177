## Appearance

At the back end of the Classic Squiffer is a light-blue bottle of cleaning spray with a blue label sticker. Said sticker has the Dolphin logo on it. The part of the spray bottle that attaches the bottle to the nozzle is blue and much longer than usual, extending almost to the end of the nozzle and then wrapping around and going almost all the way back. The handle and trigger have been combined with and situated at the back end of the attaching piece. The nozzle itself is white with supports that change color to match the user's ink.

In Splatoon 3 , the weapon's spray bottle now has a different design. There is now duct tape wrapped around the bottom and near the cap and a rubber band around the barrel and the now transparent blue frame.

## Trivia

The Classic Squiffer appears to be designed after a spray bottle.

The Classic Squiffer is one of the few weapons for which it is not apparent by its name that it is the first kit released for that weapon. Some other weapons that share this distinction are the Dark Tetra Dualies , the Nautilus 47 , Snipewriter 5H , and the Mint Decavitator .

The Squiffer was originally called "Quick Ink Rifle", as seen in an image from before Splatoon 's release, and it came with Splat Bombs and Bubbler . This set was later given to the Splattershot Jr. and Splat Bombs were given to the Splat Charger and Splatterscope .

In all three games, the Classic Squiffer has had Point Sensor as its sub weapon and various forms of a protection-based special weapon .

When Point Sensor comes up on Judd's trials , the player is equipped with the Classic Squiffer.

## Etymology

Squiffer is a portmanteau of "squid" and " Swiffer ", an American brand of cleaning products, while Classic denotes that it is the default variant of the Squiffer.
