## Appearance

The Tentatek Splattershot's appearance in each game is based on the default Splattershot variant from the same game with an added Tentatek branding.

In Splatoon , the sticker of Tentatek's oval logo is pasted on both sides of the gun's ink tank.

In Splatoon 2 , a white tape strip bearing Tentatek's logo and wordmark wraps the gun's ink tank in a diagonal position.

In Splatoon 3 , the gun's whole ink tank and its strap are recolored hot pink with a white embossed Tentatek's figure 8 logo and the brand's white wordmark beneath the logo on both sides of the ink tank.

## Trivia

From Versions 1.3.0 to 2.3.0 of Splatoon 2 , the Tentatek Splattershot's Inkjet required 230p to fully charge, being one of only two weapons in the entire Splatoon series to have had a special points requirement greater than 220p, the other weapon being the Ballpoint Splatling Nouveau in Splatoon 2 .

After its announcement for Splatoon 3 's Chill Season 2022 update, the Tentatek Splattershot's special weapon was not initially revealed. The confirmation of Triple Inkstrike 's inclusion happened indirectly before the update was released, as it appears on the weapon's Tableturf Battle card art.

This weapon is Goggles ' general weapon in the first two volumes of the Splatoon manga .

In Splatoon 3 , The Tentatek Splattershot's sub and special loadout, Splat Bomb and Triple Inkstrike , is a direct reference to the sub and special loadout of the Wasabi Splattershot in the first game .

## Etymology

Tentatek Splattershot is a combination of splatter (as well as splat ) and shot . It has the Tentatek brand name as a prefix to differentiate it from the Splattershot . It also denotes that the weapon is a collaboration with Tentatek.

Its Japanese name, スプラシューターコラボ supurashūtā korabo , is a combination of either splat or splash , and shooter . It also has the abbreviated prefix collab to separate it from Splashooter and to denote that it is a collaboration. However, unlike the English name, it does not specify the brand Tentatek.
