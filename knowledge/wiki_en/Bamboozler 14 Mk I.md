# Bamboozler 14 Mk I

Although this weapon has a very short range for a charger, its range remains constant regardless of its charge. Additionally, to compensate for its fast fire rate, the ink trail it leaves on the ground is very thin. It cannot store a fully charged shot in swim form , and its fully charged shots cannot pierce opponents, qualities most other chargers have starting in Splatoon 2 .

## Trivia

The Bamboozler 14 Mk I is a modernized version of the Bamboozler used in the Great Turf War .

Cuttlefish uses an old Bamboozler as a cane.

In Splatoon and Splatoon 3 , the Bamboozler 14 family can fire exactly fourteen fully charged shots before the user needs to refill their ink tank . Since version 5.3.0 of Splatoon 2 , the weapon can only shoot 11 fully charged shots per tank in that game.

As of version 2.1.0 of Splatoon 3 , the number of shots it can fire per tank is back to 14.

The Bamboozler has the highest charge rate of any charger in the game, excluding the Grizzco Charger .

The Bamboozler is the only charger that does not glow when fully charged.

Until the introduction of the Snipewriter 5H in Chill Season 2022 of Splatoon 3 : The Bamboozler was the only un-scoped charger to have the inability to hold a charge.

The Bamboozler was the only charger to not have the ability to splat an opponent in one shot with a full charge.

With a full set of Damage Up in Splatoon , or three mains and four subs of Main Power Up in Splatoon 2 , this weapon is able to reach 99.9 damage with only about 5/6 of a charge, or at full charge, respectively. Due to this, if an enemy steps in the player's ink, they can be splatted with only one shot.

The Bamboozler appears to be based on a type of children's toy called a bamboo gun .
