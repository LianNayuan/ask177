## Appearance

The Krak-On Splat Roller's appearance in each game is based on the default Splat Roller variant from the same game with an added Krak-On branding.

In Splatoon and Splatoon 2 , the ink tank strap has a sticker of Krak-On's wordmark and both sides of the roller cylinder have a Krak-On's circular logo each.

In Splatoon 3 , the roller frame's purple-and-yellow color scheme is changed to Krak-On's orange-and-blue and it has a white Krak-On wordmark on the left side. A sticker of Krak-On's icon-and-wordmark logo is pasted on the roller's ink tank.

## Trivia

Fitting of its name, the Krak-On Splat Roller is the first weapon to offer the Kraken special weapon in Splatoon , and the first to offer the Kraken Royale in Splatoon 3 . It is also always the first-unlocked weapon with the Squid Beakon . It is, however, not the first-unlocked weapon with the Baller, which is the Inkbrush Nouveau .

When Kraken Royale comes up in Judd's advice, the player is equipped with the Krak-On Splat Roller for the Judd's trial .

## Etymology

Krak-On Splat Roller is a combination of Splat and Roller . It has the Krak-On brand name as a prefix to differentiate it from the Splat Roller . It also denotes that the weapon is a collaboration with Krak-On.

スプラローラーコラボ supura rōrā korabo is a combination of either Splat or Splash , and Roller . It also has the abbreviated prefix Collab to separate it from Splaroller and to denote that it is a collaboration. However, unlike the English name, it does not specify the brand Krak-On.
